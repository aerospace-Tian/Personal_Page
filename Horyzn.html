Mission Pulse I: The eVTOL for defibrillator transportation in Germany.

HORYZN is a student initiative at the Technical University of Munich (TUM) focused on developing autonomous eVTOL (electric Vertical Take-Off and Landing) drones for life-saving and humanitarian applications. The project primarily aims to explore how drones can deliver automated external defibrillators (AEDs) faster than ambulances in emergency situations, particularly in cases of sudden cardiac arrest, where every second counts. At HORYZN, I was responsible for the manufacturing process of eVTOL wing structures, ensuring that the structural components met the performance and weight requirements. I actively participated in the manufacturing of carbon fiber reinforced plastic (CFRP) components, contributing to the production of lightweight and still stable aircraft parts. I also provided technical support to other teams on structural aspects and collaborated across disciplines to optimize the overall design and integration of the eVTOL drone.
